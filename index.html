About Research

"My research focuses on energy-efficient learning for neuromorphic hardware, developing bio-inspired algorithms that train and adapt directly on constrained in-memory systems under limited labels, device imperfections, and write/read consistency challenges. I am also interested in high-performance computing (GPU parallelization, CUDA), large AI models (ViT, Transformer), and reinforcement learning algorithms.

In parallel, I am also actively engaged in artistic visual practices, including photography, lino-gravure, mokulitho, and photo-gravure.

PhD Project

Three main projects during my phD.

Unsupervised End-to-End Training

A resource-efficient pseudo-labeling strategy based on winner-take-all selectivity and homeostasis, enabling unsupervised or semi-supervised training on hardware originally designed for supervised learning.

A resource-efficient pseudo-labeling strategy based on winner-take-all selectivity and homeostasis, enabling unsupervised or semi-supervised training on hardware originally designed for supervised learning.

Hardware-Aware Learning

Integration of memristor non-idealities such as nonlinear writes, variability, and noise into gradient-based training, with an emphasis on mitigating degradation through algorithmic tuning.

Dynamic Synapse Network

A framework combining volatile and non-volatile memory behavior at the synapse level to support write/read consistency with reduced circuit complexity and memory overhead.
